# Brand

Elder Council is part of the Cyber Elders family and inherits its visual system from the Elder Mind
Harness. Calm authority; honest about limits; practical over hype.

## Voice & tone

- **Calm authority.** State what the tool does and does not do, plainly. No hype, no fear-selling.
- **Honesty as a feature.** The limits ("councils can be wrong", "tamper-evident not tamper-proof",
  "not legal advice", "ships no keys") are stated up front, not buried. They are a trust differentiator.
- **Plain language over jargon.** Internal mechanics (`role_key`, `REPLACE_ME`, lane names) stay in
  the GLOSSARY and code — not in the README or CONCEPT.
- **Lead with the decision, not the machinery.** "Don't let one model decide alone" before any
  schema.

## Tagline

- Banner: **"Don't let one model decide alone."**
- Structural: **"Convene the council. Keep the dissent. Own the decision."**
- Lead cyber; domain-adaptability is a footnote, not the headline.

## Palette

| Token | Use |
|---|---|
| Indigo (authority) | primary brand, headings, the council mark |
| Green → Blue → Orange → Purple | the risk gradient (low → critical) and routing bands |
| Monospace | verdicts, decision ids, scores — anything the tool emits |

Verdict colours in the CLI: block/reject = red, escalate/defer/request-changes = amber/yellow,
human route = cyan, permitted = green. Respect `NO_COLOR` and non-TTY output.

## Typography & assets

- System-native sans + monospace — no web-font dependency (local-first ethos).
- Brand assets live in `docs/assets/` (logo, social card, demo GIF). Collateral is generated by
  `tools/build_*.py`; branded PDFs are byte-stable (pinned ReportLab) so they don't churn git.

## Claim discipline (enforced in CI)

Never: *compliant*, *certified*, *AI-powered*, *guaranteed*, *tamper-proof*, *blocks attacks*,
*covers all 10*, *councils are always right*, *eliminates bias*, *best/optimal/correct decision*,
*replaces counsel/the analyst*, *fully autonomous*. Model tags appear only inside the registry or an
explicitly illustrative context. Named regulations are illustrative and paired with "not legal advice".
